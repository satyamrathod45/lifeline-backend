Q1 (A)

Construct a frequency distribution with intervals of 1.0 hour from these data.

4.3, 2.7, 3.8, 2.2, 3.4
3.1, 4.5, 2.6, 5.5, 3.2
6.6, 2.0, 4.4, 2.1, 3.3
6.3, 6.7, 5.9, 4.1, 3.7

What conclusions can you reach about the productivity of mechanics from this distribution? If Transmission Fix-It management believes that more than 6.0 hours is evidence of unsatisfactory performance, does it have a major or minor problem with performance in this particular store?

Given

The number of hours taken by transmission mechanics to remove, repair, and replace transmissions in one of the Transmission Fix-It stores one day last week is recorded as follows:

4.3, 2.7, 3.8, 2.2, 3.4
3.1, 4.5, 2.6, 5.5, 3.2
6.6, 2.0, 4.4, 2.1, 3.3
6.3, 6.7, 5.9, 4.1, 3.7

Total observations = 20

Class Boundary Formula

Class Boundary = (Upper Class - Lower Class) / 2

3.0 − 2.9 = 0.1
Class Boundary = 0.1 / 2 = 0.05

Lower Boundary = Lower Limit − 0.05
Upper Boundary = Upper Limit + 0.05

Frequency Distribution

Class Frequency Class Boundary Midpoint
2.0 – 2.9 5 1.95 – 2.95 2.45
3.0 – 3.9 6 2.95 – 3.95 3.45
4.0 – 4.9 4 3.95 – 4.95 4.45
5.0 – 5.9 2 4.95 – 5.95 5.45
6.0 – 6.9 3 5.95 – 6.95 6.45
Total 20

Conclusion

Transmission Fix-It management considers more than 6 hours as unsatisfactory performance.

Mechanics taking more than 6 hours = 3

Percentage of unsatisfactory performance:

(3 / 20) × 100 = 15%

Since only 15% of mechanics take more than 6 hours, the store has a minor performance problem, not a major one.

Question 1 (B)

National Tire Company holds reserve funds in short-term marketable securities. The ending daily balance (in millions) of the marketable securities account for 2 weeks is shown below.

Week 1: 1.973   1.970   1.972   1.975   1.976
Week 2: 1.969   1.892   1.893   1.887   1.895

Find the average (mean) amount invested in marketable securities during:
(a) The first week
(b) The second week
(c) The two-week period
(d) Determine if the company qualifies for higher interest rates
(e) If the average is less than 1.970 million, by how much must the last day increase
(f) If the average was greater than 1.970 million, how much could be withdrawn

Solution

(a) Mean for Week 1

Mean = (Sum of Observations) / Number of Observations

Mean = (1.973 + 1.970 + 1.972 + 1.975 + 1.976) / 5

Mean = 9.866 / 5

Mean = 1.9732 million

(b) Mean for Week 2

Mean = (1.969 + 1.892 + 1.893 + 1.887 + 1.895) / 5

Mean = 9.536 / 5

Mean = 1.9072 million

(c) Mean for Two Weeks

Total Sum = 9.866 + 9.536

Total Sum = 19.402

Mean = 19.402 / 10

Mean = 1.9402 million

(d) Qualification for Higher Interest Rate

Required Average Balance = 1.970 million

Calculated Average = 1.9402 million

Since 1.9402 < 1.970, the company does NOT qualify for the higher interest rate.

(e) Increase Required in Last Day Investment

Required Total = 1.970 × 10

Required Total = 19.70

Current Total = 19.402

Increase Required = 19.70 − 19.402

Increase = 0.298 million

(f) Withdrawal if Average Was Above 1.970

If the average balance had been greater than 1.970 million, the company treasurer could withdraw the difference between the actual total and the required total (19.70 million) while still maintaining the qualifying average balance.

Question 1 (C)

Which of the following are mutually exclusive outcomes in the rolling of two dice?

(a) A total of 5 points and a 5 on one die.
(b) A total of 7 points and an even number of points on both dice.
(c) A total of 8 points and an odd number of points on both dice.
(d) A total of 9 points and a 2 on one die.
(e) A total of 10 points and a 4 on one die.

Solution

Mutually exclusive events are events that cannot occur at the same time.

(a) Total of 5 and a 5 on one die

If one die shows 5, the smallest possible total is 6. Therefore total 5 is impossible.

These events are mutually exclusive.

(b) Total of 7 and both numbers even

Even numbers on dice are 2, 4, and 6. The sum of two even numbers is always even, but 7 is odd.

These events are mutually exclusive.

(c) Total of 8 and both numbers odd

Possible odd numbers are 1, 3, and 5. Odd + odd gives an even number and combinations like (3,5) or (5,3) give total 8.

Therefore these events can occur together.

These events are NOT mutually exclusive.

(d) Total of 9 and a 2 on one die

If one die is 2, the other must be 7 to make total 9, which is impossible on a die.

These events are mutually exclusive.

(e) Total of 10 and a 4 on one die

Possible combinations for total 10 include (4,6) and (6,4).

Both conditions can occur together.

These events are NOT mutually exclusive.

Final Answer

Mutually Exclusive Events: (a), (b), and (d)

Question 2 (A)

The latest nationwide political poll indicates that for Indians who are randomly selected, the probability that they are with alliance ABC is 0.55, the probability that they are with alliance PQR is 0.30, and the probability that they are with alliance XYZ is 0.15. Assuming that these binomial probabilities are accurate, answer the following questions pertaining to a randomly chosen group of 10 Indians.

(a) What is the probability that four are with alliance PQR?
(b) What is the probability that none are with alliance ABC?
(c) What is the probability that two are with alliance XYZ?
(d) What is the probability that at least eight are with alliance PQR?

Solution

Binomial Probability Formula

P(r) = ( n! / ( r!(n − r)! ) ) pr(q)n−r

(a)

n = 10 , p = 0.30

P(r = 4) = (10! / (4!6!)) (0.30)4(0.70)6

= 210 × 0.0081 × 0.117649

P(r = 4) = 0.2001

(b)

n = 10 , p = 0.55

P(r = 0) = (10! / (0!10!)) (0.55)0(0.45)10

P(r = 0) = 0.0003

(c)

n = 10 , p = 0.15

P(r = 2) = (10! / (2!8!)) (0.15)2(0.85)8

= 45 × 0.0225 × 0.27249

P(r = 2) = 0.2759

(d)

n = 10 , p = 0.30

P(r = 8) + P(r = 9) + P(r = 10)

P(r = 8) = (10! / (8!2!)) (0.30)8(0.70)2

P(r = 9) = (10! / (9!1!)) (0.30)9(0.70)1

P(r = 10) = (10! / (10!0!)) (0.30)10(0.70)0

= 0.00145 + 0.00014 + 0.00001

P(r ≥ 8) = 0.0016

Question 2 (B)

If the prices of new cars increase an average of four times every 3 years, find the probability of:

(a) No price hikes in a randomly selected period of 3 years.
(b) Two price hikes.
(c) Four price hikes.
(d) Five or more price hikes.

Solution

Poisson Probability Formula

P(r) = ( e-λ λr ) / r!

(a)

λ = 4

P(r = 0) = ( e-4 40 ) / 0!

= 0.0183

(b)

P(r = 2) = ( e-4 42 ) / 2!

= (0.0183 × 16) / 2

P(r = 2) = 0.1465

(c)

P(r = 4) = ( e-4 44 ) / 4!

= (0.0183 × 256) / 24

P(r = 4) = 0.1954

(d)

P(r ≥ 5) = 1 − [P(0)+P(1)+P(2)+P(3)+P(4)]

= 1 − (0.0183 + 0.0733 + 0.1465 + 0.1954 + 0.1954)

P(r ≥ 5) = 0.3711

Question 2 (C)

Parminder Singh is the supervisor for the Krishna Hydroelectric Dam. The turbines generate electricity at the peak rate only when at least 1,000,000 gallons of water pass through the dam each day.

The daily flow is normally distributed with the mean equal to the previous day's flow and a standard deviation of 200,000 gallons. Yesterday 850,000 gallons flowed through the dam.

What is the probability that the turbines will generate at peak rate today?

Solution

For today,

μ = 850,000      σ = 200,000

P(X ≥ 1,000,000)

= P(Z ≥ (1,000,000 − 850,000) / 200,000)

= P(Z ≥ 0.75)

Using Z Table:

Area between mean and Z = 0.75 = 0.2734

P(Z ≥ 0.75)

= 0.5 − 0.2734

P(X ≥ 1,000,000) = 0.2266

(a) What is P(92 < x̄ < 102)?
(b) Find the corresponding probability given a sample of 36.

Solution

(a)    n = 25    μ = 98.6    σ = 17.2

σx̄ = σ / √n

= 17.2 / √25

= 3.44

P(92 < x̄ < 102)

= P((92 − 98.6) / 3.44 < (x̄ − μ)/σx̄ < (102 − 98.6) / 3.44)

= P(−1.92 < Z < 0.99)

Using Z Table:

= 0.4726 + 0.3389

= 0.8115
(b)    n = 36

σx̄ = σ / √n

= 17.2 / √36

= 2.87

P(92 < x̄ < 102)

= P((92 − 98.6) / 2.87 < (x̄ − μ)/σx̄ < (102 − 98.6) / 2.87)

= P(−2.30 < Z < 1.18)

Using Z Table:

= 0.4893 + 0.3810

= 0.8703

Question 3 (C)

Food Place, a chain of 145 supermarkets, has been bought by a larger nationwide chain. Management claims that store profits follow a normal distribution with the same mean and a standard deviation of $1200.

If 36 stores are randomly selected, what is the probability that the sample mean will fall within $200 of the actual mean?

Solution

N = 145    n = 36    σ = 1200

σx̄ = (σ / √n) √((N − n)/(N − 1))

= (1200 / √36) √((145 − 36)/(145 − 1))

= (1200 / 6) √(109 / 144)

= 200 × 0.8699

σx̄ ≈ 173.98

Required probability:

P( μ − 200 < x̄ < μ + 200 )

= P((−200 / 173.98) < Z < (200 / 173.98))

= P(−1.15 < Z < 1.15)

Using Z Table:

Area (0 → 1.15) = 0.3749

P(−1.15 < Z < 1.15)

= 2 × 0.3749

= 0.7498
